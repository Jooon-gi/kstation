Key Ingredients & Concentration

Polydioxanone (PDO) Microspheres - 200mg

This product contains 200mg of Polydioxanone (PDO) micro-spheres in lyophilized powder form. PDO is a synthetic absorbable material widely used in surgical sutures, known for its excellent biocompatibility and ability to stimulate collagen production (neocollagenesis) as it naturally degrades over time.

Reconstitution Requirements

The PDO micro-spheres are mixed with a sterile diluent (such as Saline and/or Lidocaine solution, prepared by the practitioner) prior to injection.

Note on Lidocaine: This product does not inherently contain Lidocaine. Lidocaine is typically added by the administering practitioner during the reconstitution process for patient comfort.

Mechanism of Action & Technical Features

When injected, the PDO micro-spheres create a scaffold that gradually stimulates fibroblasts to produce new collagen around them. As the PDO material naturally absorbs over 6-8 months, the newly generated collagen remains, sustaining the rejuvenating effect.

Particle Size
Uniform and optimized micro-sphere size for smooth injection, even distribution, and maximized collagen stimulation surface area
Microsphere Technology Advantage
Offers advantages over traditional PDO thread treatments by allowing for a more uniform and widespread distribution of the collagen-stimulating material across a larger treatment plane

Protocol & Instructions

Injection Layer by Treatment Area
Mid-to-deep dermis, or Subcutaneous layer, depending on the targeted correction (e.g., deep wrinkles vs. overall volume)
Recommended Needle Gauge
Typically 27G or 30G needle, or a Cannula (e.g., 25G or 27G), depending on the area, injection depth, and practitioner's technique
Treatment Cycle
A primary treatment followed by 1-2 additional sessions, usually spaced 4-8 weeks apart, may be recommended for optimal results. Maintenance is generally recommended every 12-18 months

Which Product is Right for Me?

Ultracol is a single product that can be tailored for different needs by adjusting the dilution ratio (affecting "viscosity") and the injection technique.

For Structural Volume & Lifting

A less diluted solution injected into the Sub-Q layer is preferred.

For Overall Skin Texture & Fine Wrinkle Improvement

A more diluted solution injected superficially into the dermis is recommended.
