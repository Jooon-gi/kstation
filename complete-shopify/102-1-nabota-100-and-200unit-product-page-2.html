Usage Protocol & Treatment Guidelines

Recommended Dilution

Reconstitution should be done with sterile, preservative-free 0.9% Sodium Chloride Injection. Slowly inject the diluent against the vial wall to avoid foaming.

Recommended Units by Treatment Area

  • Glabellar Lines: Typically 20 units total, divided into 5 injection points
  • Crow's Feet: 12-24 units per side
  • Masseter Muscles: 50-60 units per side
Onset of Effect
Typically observed within 2-3 days, with maximum results visible after 1-2 weeks
Duration
Generally 3-6 months or more, with re-treatment recommended no more frequently than every three months

Mechanism of Action

How It Works

Botulinum Toxin Type A works by blocking nerve signals to muscles, causing temporary muscle relaxation and reducing the appearance of wrinkles and treating various medical conditions.

[Infographic - Botulinum Toxin Mechanism of Action]

Precautions & Storage

Important Safety Information — Do not use on pregnant or nursing mothers, or individuals with known hypersensitivity to any botulinum toxin preparation or component.

Storage Requirements

  • Store in refrigerator at 2-8°C
  • Keep in sealed container
  • Do not freeze the product
  • Use within 24 hours after reconstitution

Safety Guidelines

  • Single-dose use only - discard any unused portion
  • Always consult with qualified medical professional
  • Check for hypersensitivity before treatment
  • Professional administration required
  • Follow proper injection techniques
