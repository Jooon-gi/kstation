Application Protocol

Injection Technique
Use appropriate needle gauge and inject into designated layer. Massage gently to ensure even distribution.

Provide aftercare instructions including ice application and activity restrictions for optimal healing.

Safety Information

Professional Use Only — This product is intended for use by qualified medical professionals trained in dermal filler injection techniques.

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Known allergy to hyaluronic acid or lidocaine
  • Active skin infection at treatment site
  • Autoimmune disorders
  • History of severe allergic reactions

Precautions

  • Avoid use if package is damaged
  • Do not use beyond expiration date
  • Single-use only - do not resterilize
  • Perform skin test if patient sensitivity is suspected
  • Monitor patients for adverse reactions

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes ELASTY different from other HA fillers? +
ELASTY utilizes PNET™ (Preserved Natural Entanglement Technology) with BDDE cross-linking, which provides superior elasticity and cohesion while maintaining natural tissue integration. The monophasic gel structure ensures smooth injection and long-lasting results.
Which ELASTY product should I choose for my patient? +
Selection depends on treatment goals: ELASTY G for structural volume enhancement and deep contouring, ELASTY D for moderate to deep wrinkles and lip augmentation, and ELASTY F for fine lines and superficial corrections. Consider injection depth and area when making your choice.
How long do ELASTY results typically last? +
Duration varies by product and individual factors. ELASTY G typically lasts 12-18 months, while ELASTY D and F generally provide results for 8-12 months. Factors affecting longevity include injection technique, treatment area, patient metabolism, and lifestyle factors.
Is special training required for ELASTY injection? +
Yes, proper training in dermal filler injection techniques is essential. Healthcare professionals should be familiar with facial anatomy, injection depths, and proper needle selection. Consider attending specialized training courses for optimal results and patient safety.
