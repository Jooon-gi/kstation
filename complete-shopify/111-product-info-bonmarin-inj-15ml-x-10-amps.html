‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Who should consider BONMARIN injection treatment?

A. BONMARIN injection is ideal for individuals with diagnosed vitamin D deficiency, those at risk of osteoporosis, or patients with conditions that impair vitamin D absorption from oral supplements. Medical assessment is required to determine suitability.

Q. How long do the effects of one injection last?

A. The effects of a single BONMARIN injection may last several months, as indicated by the high-dose formulation. The exact duration varies by individual and should be monitored through blood level testing.

Q. Can I administer this injection myself?

A. No, BONMARIN injection must be administered by qualified healthcare professionals only. This ensures proper technique, dosage determination, and monitoring for adverse reactions.

Q. What blood tests are needed before treatment?

A. Your healthcare provider will test your 25-hydroxy vitamin D levels to assess deficiency and determine appropriate dosage. Additional tests may be required based on your medical history.

Q. Are there any side effects I should be aware of?

A. When administered properly under medical supervision, side effects are rare. However, overdose can cause symptoms such as headache, muscle pain, nausea, and vomiting. Report any unusual symptoms to your healthcare provider immediately.

Q. How should unused ampoules be stored?

A. Store unopened ampoules in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Once opened, the contents must be used immediately and cannot be stored for later use.
