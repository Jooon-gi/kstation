Key Ingredients & Formulation

Deoxycholic Acid
Active Ingredient
A bile acid naturally occurring in the human body that aids in the breakdown and absorption of dietary fat. When injected, it directly destroys fat cells (adipocytes), which are then naturally metabolized and eliminated by the body. This process permanently removes the fat cells in the treated area and prevents new fat accumulation.
Mechanism of Action
Deoxycholic acid causes cytolytic destruction of adipocytes through cell membrane disruption, leading to permanent fat cell elimination and natural metabolic clearance.
Formulation Technology
Advanced stabilized formulation ensures optimal bioavailability and consistent performance across diverse treatment applications.
Molecular Structure
Naturally occurring bile acid with proven safety profile and established efficacy in fat cell destruction and removal.

Safety Information & Precautions

Important Safety Guidelines
Do not use in areas with infection or inflammation. Use with caution in patients with bleeding abnormalities or those on antiplatelet or anticoagulant therapy. Avoid injecting into or in close proximity (1-1.5 cm) to salivary glands, lymph nodes, and muscles. Follow proper injection techniques to avoid superficial layers or vulnerable anatomical structures.
Contraindications & Precautions
To avoid potential nerve injury, injection should not be done into or in close proximity to the marginal mandibular branch of the facial nerve. Ensure proper anatomical knowledge before treatment. Use appropriate syringe gauge and maintain sterile technique throughout procedure.
Common Side Effects
Swelling, bruising, pain, numbness, redness, and hardness at the injection site are common but generally temporary. Seek medical advice if symptoms persist or if you experience an uneven smile, facial muscle weakness, or difficulty swallowing. Post-treatment swelling and bruising are expected and will resolve naturally.
Treatment Intervals
Adhere to recommended interval of at least 1 week between sessions to allow for resolution of post-injection swelling and to avoid over-treatment.
Professional Administration
Should only be administered by qualified healthcare professionals with proper training in injection techniques and facial anatomy.
Post-Treatment Monitoring
Monitor patients for immediate and delayed reactions. Provide clear aftercare instructions and emergency contact information.
Emergency Procedures
Have appropriate emergency protocols in place for adverse reactions. Ensure access to medical support and intervention if needed.
