Mechanism of Action

Fursultiamine is converted to thiamine in the body, where it acts as a coenzyme in carbohydrate metabolism. It plays a crucial role in the citric acid cycle and pentose phosphate pathway, essential for cellular energy production and proper neurological function.

Safety Information & Precautions

Important Safety Guidelines
HAPROKIN inj is a prescription medication that must be administered by qualified healthcare professionals. Proper safety protocols must be followed during preparation and administration.
  • Do not use if hypersensitive to fursultiamine or any component
  • Administer IV injections slowly to prevent vascular pain
  • Handle glass ampoules carefully to prevent glass fragment contamination
  • Discontinue immediately if hypersensitivity reactions occur
  • Monitor for rare but serious allergic reactions including shock
  • Discard unused solution from opened ampoules immediately

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to fursultiamine hydrochloride or any excipients. Use with caution in patients with history of severe allergic reactions to B-complex vitamins.

Emergency Response

In case of hypersensitivity reactions, discontinue injection immediately. Have emergency medications readily available. Monitor vital signs and provide supportive care as needed. Contact emergency services for severe reactions.
