Treatment Protocol & Application Methods

Direct Injection Method
Professional application via direct dermal injection using 32G needle. Recommended injection interval of 1 cm in target areas for optimal distribution and effectiveness. Suitable for precise treatment of specific problem areas.
Micro-needling (MTS) Method
Application through micro-needling techniques for enhanced penetration and patient comfort. This method allows for broader area coverage while maintaining treatment efficacy and minimizing discomfort.
Treatment Cycle Protocol
Recommended 3-5 treatment sessions spaced 14 days apart for optimal results. This protocol ensures proper healing time between sessions while maintaining progressive improvement in treated areas.
Multi-Area Application
Versatile application for under-eye area, crow's feet, neck, and back of hands. Each area requires specific technique adaptation to ensure optimal results and patient safety throughout treatment.
Treatment Frequency
Standard protocol: 3-5 sessions with 14-day intervals between treatments. Maintenance treatments may be scheduled every 3-6 months based on individual patient needs.
Application Technique
Use 32G needle for direct injection or appropriate micro-needling device. Maintain sterile technique and follow standard injection protocols for optimal safety and efficacy.
Target Areas
Primary: Under-eye area and crow's feet. Secondary: Neck and back of hands. Adapt injection depth and technique based on skin thickness in each area.

Safety Information & Precautions

Professional Use Only
This product is intended for use by qualified medical professionals only. Proper training in injection techniques and knowledge of facial anatomy required. Do not use on broken or irritated skin. Follow all professional guidelines for treatment protocols.
Treatment Considerations
Assess patient suitability before treatment. Consider contraindications including pregnancy, breastfeeding, active infections, or known allergies to ingredients. Inform patients of potential temporary side effects including redness, swelling, or minor bruising at injection sites.
Storage & Handling Safety
Maintain cold chain storage requirements. Use sterile technique throughout preparation and application. Dispose of used materials according to medical waste regulations. Keep detailed treatment records for patient safety and follow-up care.
Contraindications
Do not use in patients with known hypersensitivity to any ingredients, active skin infections, or autoimmune conditions affecting the treatment area.
Potential Side Effects
Temporary redness, swelling, bruising, or tenderness at injection sites. These effects typically resolve within 24-48 hours post-treatment.
Emergency Procedures
In case of adverse reaction, discontinue treatment immediately and provide appropriate medical care. Have emergency protocols readily available during all procedures.
