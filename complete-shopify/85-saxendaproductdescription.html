Usage Instructions & Dose Escalation

Step-by-Step Application Guide
1
Week 1: Starting Dose (0.6 mg)
Begin with 0.6 mg daily subcutaneous injection. Rotate injection sites between abdomen, thigh, or upper arm to prevent lipodystrophy.
2
Weeks 2-5: Gradual Increase
Increase dose by 0.6 mg each week: Week 2 (1.2 mg), Week 3 (1.8 mg), Week 4 (2.4 mg), Week 5 (3.0 mg maintenance dose).
3
Daily Administration
Inject once daily at any time of day, with or without food. Maintain consistent timing for optimal results.
4
Injection Technique
Use proper subcutaneous injection technique. Attach new needle for each injection and dispose of safely according to local regulations.
5
Lifestyle Integration
Combine with reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity for maximum effectiveness.

Important Usage Notes

  • If a dose is missed and more than 12 hours have passed, skip the missed dose and resume normal schedule the following day
  • The dose escalation schedule is crucial to minimize gastrointestinal side effects
  • Users must be trained on proper injection technique and dose-setting before first use
  • Single-use needles are required for each injection (needles not included with pen)

Safety Information & Important Warnings

Critical Safety Guidelines
Do not use if you have a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2. Not recommended for patients with a history of pancreatitis. Always consult healthcare provider before starting treatment.
Pre-Treatment Requirements
Medical evaluation required to confirm BMI eligibility and assess for contraindications. Patients must be trained on proper injection technique and dose escalation protocol before first use.
Common Side Effects
Most common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and injection site reactions. These typically decrease over time as the body adjusts to treatment. Dose escalation helps minimize gastrointestinal effects.
Product Handling Safety
Never freeze the pen. Do not use if solution is not clear and colorless. Do not share pens with others due to risk of infection transmission. Dispose of needles and pens according to local medical waste guidelines.

Emergency Response Protocol

Severe Adverse Reactions

Contact healthcare provider immediately if experiencing severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, or signs of allergic reaction including rash, swelling, or difficulty breathing.

Medical Support

Ensure 24/7 access to healthcare provider contact information. Report any unexpected symptoms or concerns during treatment period.

Precautions & Contraindications

Important Medical Considerations

Absolute Contraindications

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
  • Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
  • Previous serious hypersensitivity reaction to liraglutide or any excipients
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding (not studied in these populations)

Special Populations Requiring Caution

Diabetic Patients

Monitor blood glucose closely, especially if taking insulin or sulfonylureas, as dose adjustments may be necessary to prevent hypoglycemia.

Kidney/Liver Impairment

Use with caution in patients with renal or hepatic impairment. Dose adjustment may be required based on severity of condition.

Elderly Patients

Limited experience in patients over 75 years. Start with lower doses and monitor more frequently for adverse effects.

Gallbladder Disease

May increase risk of gallbladder-related events. Monitor patients with history of gallstones or gallbladder disease.
