Safety Information & Precautions

Important Safety Information
Professional administration recommended. Carefully review patient history and contraindications before use.
  • Do not administer to patients with a predisposition to allergies without professional consultation
  • Use caution when injecting to avoid nerves and blood vessels
  • If a rash or other allergic symptoms occur, discontinue use immediately
  • Risk of transmitting infectious agents, although highly minimized by sterilization and screening, cannot be entirely eliminated

Storage Guidelines

Store in an opaque, sealed container at room temperature (1-30°C). Protect from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Check expiration date before use.

Professional Use Only

This product is intended for use by qualified healthcare professionals only. Proper training in injection techniques and emergency response procedures is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

LAENNEC can be administered via subcutaneous, intramuscular, intravenous, or intradermal injection depending on the therapeutic goal. Subcutaneous injection is most common for general regenerative therapy, while intradermal injections are preferred for skin rejuvenation treatments.
For general therapeutic use, the standard dose is 2mL once daily. For skin rejuvenation, a typical protocol involves twice-weekly injections for 4 weeks, followed by weekly maintenance doses. Treatment frequency may be adjusted based on individual response and medical supervision.
Patients with known allergies to placental extracts should avoid use. Those with compromised immune systems or autoimmune conditions should be evaluated carefully. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider before starting treatment.
Store in opaque, sealed containers at room temperature between 1-30°C. Protect from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Do not freeze. Check expiration dates before use and dispose of any damaged ampoules.
Most patients experience minimal discomfort during injection. Initial improvements may be noticed within 1-2 weeks, with more significant results typically seen after 4-6 weeks of treatment. Some patients may experience mild injection site reactions, which usually resolve quickly.
