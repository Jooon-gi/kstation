PILLA PLLA Dermal Filler

Long-Lasting Volume Enhancement Through Neocollagenesis

PILLA PLLA Dermal Filler is a biocompatible and biodegradable poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA) based injectable that stimulates natural collagen production for gradual volume restoration and skin quality improvement.

Key Feature: Unlike traditional fillers, PILLA works by triggering the body's natural collagen production process, leading to results that improve over time and can last up to 2 years or more.

Mechanism of Action

Understanding how PLLA stimulates natural collagen formation
PLLA Injection
When injected into the deep dermis or sub-dermal layer, PLLA microparticles are distributed throughout the treatment area.
Controlled Absorption
The PLLA microparticles are gradually absorbed by the body, triggering a mild inflammatory response.
Fibroblast Activation
This response stimulates fibroblast activity, the cells responsible for collagen production.
Neocollagenesis
New collagen formation occurs over weeks to months, gradually restoring volume and improving skin quality.

Treatment Protocol

Professional reconstitution and injection guidelines
Important: PILLA PLLA is a lyophilized powder that must be reconstituted prior to injection. For use by qualified, licensed medical professionals only.
Reconstitution Volume
Typically requires 5mL to 8mL of sterile water for injection, plus 1mL to 2mL of 1% Lidocaine (as recommended by the physician). Must be mixed 24-72 hours before treatment for full hydration.
Injection Depth
Sub-dermal layer or deep dermis. Avoid superficial injection to minimize risk of nodule formation.
Recommended Needle/Cannula
Typically 26G or 27G cannula for general facial contouring and volume enhancement.
Injection Technique
Fanning or linear threading technique to ensure even dispersion throughout the treatment area.
Treatment Cycle
A complete treatment generally consists of 2 to 4 sessions, spaced approximately 4 to 6 weeks apart for optimal results.

Strategy Selection Guide

PILLA is a single-formulation product. The optimal strategy involves adjusting the volume of sterile water and Lidocaine used for reconstitution (which affects dispersion and thickness) and the injection depth to match the treatment goal.

  • Higher reconstitution volumes (9-10mL) are used for more superficial and diffuse treatments focused on skin quality improvement
  • Lower volumes (6-7mL) are used for localized volume correction in deep folds and targeted areas

Duration of Results

Progressive & Long-Lasting Enhancement

Results from PILLA PLLA are not immediate. Volume enhancement becomes visible gradually over weeks to months as neocollagenesis occurs.

  • Initial Changes: Visible improvements typically begin 4-6 weeks after the first treatment session
  • Progressive Development: Results continue to improve over 3-6 months as collagen production increases
  • Duration: Results can last up to 2 years or more after the final treatment session, as the PLLA is absorbed and replaced by the body's own collagen
  • Maintenance: Touch-up treatments may be recommended to maintain optimal results

Post-Treatment Care

Essential guidelines for optimal results
Critical: Post-treatment massage is crucial for even product distribution and minimizing nodule formation.

The Rule of 5 for Massage

Patients should massage the treated area following this protocol:

  • 5 times per day
  • For 5 minutes each session
  • For 5 days following treatment

Proper massage technique ensures even distribution of the product and helps prevent nodule formation.

Expected Side Effects

Temporary side effects may include:

  • Redness at injection sites
  • Mild swelling
  • Bruising
  • Tenderness or sensitivity

These effects typically resolve within a few days to one week.

Activities to Avoid

For the first 24-48 hours after treatment:

  • Avoid excessive sun exposure
  • Avoid extreme temperatures (sauna, hot tub)
  • Avoid strenuous exercise
  • Avoid excessive facial movements in treated areas

Storage & Handling

Storage Temperature
Store at room temperature (below 30°C or 86°F). Do not freeze.
Shelf Life After Reconstitution
Once reconstituted, the product must typically be used within 72 hours. Manufacturer-specific instructions should be strictly followed.
Handling Precautions
Product must be stored in original packaging until use. Avoid exposure to direct sunlight.

Safety Information & Precautions

Professional Use Only: For use by qualified, licensed medical professionals only. Requires proper reconstitution and knowledge of injection techniques for PLLA products.

Critical Safety Warnings

  • Strictly avoid injection into blood vessels
  • Not recommended for thin skin areas (lips, under eyes)
  • Proper injection depth is essential to minimize nodule risk
  • Post-treatment massage is mandatory
  • Patients must be informed of gradual result timeline

Contraindications

  • Active skin infections or inflammation at treatment site
  • Known hypersensitivity to PLLA or any component
  • History of keloid formation or hypertrophic scarring
  • Patients with bleeding disorders
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding

Professional Requirements

Healthcare providers should have:

  • Comprehensive knowledge of facial anatomy
  • Experience with PLLA-based products
  • Understanding of reconstitution protocols
  • Proficiency in cannula and needle injection techniques
  • Ability to educate patients on expected timeline and post-care
