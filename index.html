Storage & Safety Information

Storage Requirements

  • Store at controlled room temperature: 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F)
  • Do not refrigerate or freeze
  • Protect from light exposure
  • Shake well before each use

Important Precautions

  • Contains benzyl alcohol - caution with neonatal use
  • Use with caution in heart failure, hypertension, renal insufficiency
  • Avoid injection into infected sites or unstable joints
  • Gradual dose reduction required after long-term therapy
Contraindications — NOT for intravenous, intradermal, intraocular, or intrathecal use.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the recommended frequency of administration?
Administration frequency typically ranges from every 3-4 weeks, but should be determined individually by a healthcare professional based on the specific condition being treated and patient response.
Can this be mixed with other medications?
Triamcinolone may be used with local anesthetics like lidocaine for patient comfort during injection. However, any medication combinations should only be made under professional medical guidance.
What should be done if the suspension appears settled?
Always shake the vial well before use to ensure uniform distribution of the active ingredient. If the suspension appears severely separated or contains particles, consult pharmaceutical guidelines before use.
Are there specific patient populations that require extra caution?
Yes, use with particular caution in patients with heart failure, hypertension, renal insufficiency, and neonates due to benzyl alcohol content. Individual assessment is essential for each patient.
